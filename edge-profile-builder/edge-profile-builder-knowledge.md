# EDGE Profile Builder Knowledge

## Purpose

EDGE Profile Builder creates a reusable file called:

`learner-profile.md`

This file can be used as personalised context/instructions in AI tools like:

- ChatGPT
- Claude
- Gemini
- Perplexity
- other LLMs

The goal is to help AI understand the user’s:

- current environment
- direction
- gaps
- engagement style

This improves the quality of AI responses because the AI is no longer responding to a stranger.

---

# EDGE Framework

## E — Environment

Question it answers:

“Where am I right now?”

Capture:

- current role or identity
- current life/professional stage
- normal week
- responsibilities
- constraints
- pressure
- available time and energy
- what feels unclear, heavy, or urgent
- what feels underused or invisible

Good examples:

- “I am 7 years into operations and feel capable but stuck.”
- “Most of my week goes into follow-ups, coordination, and issue resolution.”
- “I have 4–5 hours a week for serious improvement.”
- “I feel my ability to bring structure to messy work is underused.”

Weak examples:

- “I am working.”
- “I am busy.”
- “I want growth.”
- “I do many things.”

---

## D — Direction

Question it answers:

“Where am I trying to go?”

Capture:

- what matters now
- what the user wants to change
- what they want more control over
- what they want to build, improve, or become
- what they are tired of repeating
- what they are no longer willing to tolerate
- 3–6 month success picture
- longer-term direction

Good examples:

- “I want to move from silent execution to being recognised for decision-quality work.”
- “I want more control over income and career direction.”
- “I am tired of working hard quietly and waiting for someone else to notice.”
- “In 6 months, I want to communicate my value clearly and use AI as a thinking partner.”

Weak examples:

- “I want success.”
- “I want growth.”
- “I want financial freedom.”
- “I want to become better.”

Pushback:

If user says “growth,” ask:
“Growth can mean money, clarity, confidence, better role, independence, health, skills, or visibility. Which 2 are closest?”

If user says “financial freedom,” ask:
“Do you mean debt reduction, higher income, second income, less job dependence, better savings, or freedom to choose work?”

---

## G — Gaps

Question it answers:

“What is blocking or slowing me down?”

Capture:

- skill gaps
- confidence gaps
- clarity gaps
- execution gaps
- decision-making gaps
- communication gaps
- avoidance patterns
- overthinking
- delayed action
- situations where the user freezes, rushes, over-explains, avoids, or stays silent
- what AI should help improve but not do completely for them

Good examples:

- “I lose confidence when speaking to senior people.”
- “I overthink before posting or applying for opportunities.”
- “I delay action when the next step is visible to others.”
- “I explain tasks but struggle to explain value.”
- “I want AI to help me structure my thinking, not make decisions for me.”

Weak examples:

- “I lack confidence.”
- “I overthink.”
- “I procrastinate.”
- “I need discipline.”
- “I want better communication.”

Pushback:

If user says “I lack confidence,” ask:
“Where does confidence drop: speaking, writing, deciding, asking, presenting, negotiating, applying, posting, or leading?”

If user says “I overthink,” ask:
“What do you overthink most: messages, decisions, career moves, conversations, content, money, relationships, or starting something?”

If user says “I want better communication,” ask:
“Do you mean explaining ideas, writing clearly, speaking in meetings, handling conflict, asking questions, presenting yourself, or saying difficult things calmly?”

---

## E — Engagement

Question it answers:

“How should AI work with me?”

Capture:

- preferred explanation style
- preferred response depth
- feedback style
- how much pushback they want
- best tone
- tone to avoid
- preferred AI role
- what AI should do when the user is stuck
- what AI should never do

Possible AI roles:

- Coach: helps user improve through questions and guidance
- Strategist: helps think through direction, trade-offs, and decisions
- Teacher: explains concepts clearly
- Editor: improves writing and communication
- Sparring partner: challenges assumptions
- Accountability partner: helps convert intention into action

Good examples:

- “Explain simply with examples first.”
- “Be direct but not harsh.”
- “Push back when I give vague answers.”
- “When I am stuck, simplify the next step.”
- “Do not give fake motivation.”
- “Do not overpraise weak answers.”
- “Do not write everything for me before helping me think.”

Weak examples:

- “Help me.”
- “Give good answers.”
- “Motivate me.”
- “Be friendly.”

Pushback:

If user says “I want AI to help me,” ask:
“Should AI help you think, write, decide, plan, practice, organise, challenge you, or hold you accountable?”

# Question Bank

## Environment Questions

1. What is your current role, work, or main identity right now?
2. What stage are you in professionally or personally?
3. What does your normal week mostly involve?
4. What responsibilities are you carrying right now?
5. What currently feels unclear, heavy, or urgent?
6. What part of your capability feels underused or invisible?
7. How much time and energy do you realistically have for learning, building, or improving something?
8. What are you responsible for that others may not fully see?
9. What pressure are you carrying that affects how you work?

## Direction Questions

1. What are you trying to move toward in the next 3–6 months?
2. What do you want more control over?
3. What are you tired of repeating?
4. What do you want to become better at?
5. What future version of yourself feels attractive but slightly uncomfortable?
6. What are you no longer willing to tolerate?
7. What would make the next few months feel meaningful or successful?
8. What do you want your work or life to feel less dependent on?
9. What are you building toward, even if it is not fully clear yet?

## Gaps Questions

1. Where do you currently feel stuck or blocked?
2. What do you keep delaying even though you know it matters?
3. Where do you lose confidence?
4. In which situations do you overthink?
5. What conversations, decisions, or actions make you uncomfortable?
6. What pattern keeps repeating that you want to break?
7. What do you usually avoid?
8. What do you want AI to help improve, but not do completely for you?
9. Where do you need more clarity before taking action?
10. Where do you wait for external validation?

## Engagement Questions

1. How do you want AI to explain things: short/direct, detailed, examples-first, step-by-step, or with reasoning?
2. When your answer is vague, do you want AI to push back?
3. Do you prefer AI to ask questions first or suggest an answer first?
4. What tone helps you take action?
5. What tone irritates you or makes you shut down?
6. Should AI act more like a coach, strategist, teacher, editor, sparring partner, or accountability partner?
7. When you are stuck, should AI simplify the task, give examples, challenge the excuse, or help choose one next step?
8. What should AI never do while helping you?
9. Do you prefer options first, recommendation first, or reasoning first?
10. Do you want concise answers or deeper explanations?

# Generic Answer Pushback Examples

## Generic: “I want growth.”

Response:
“That is a useful start, but ‘growth’ is still broad. Do you mean more money, more confidence, better role, more independence, stronger skills, better health, clearer thinking, or more visibility? Pick the closest 2.”

## Generic: “I want clarity.”

Response:
“Clarity about what specifically: career, next step, daily priorities, offer, communication, confidence, relationships, money, or long-term direction?”

## Generic: “I lack confidence.”

Response:
“Where does confidence drop most: speaking, writing, deciding, asking, presenting, negotiating, applying, posting, or leading? Pick the top 2.”

## Generic: “I overthink.”

Response:
“What do you overthink most: messages, decisions, career moves, conversations, content, money, relationships, or starting something?”

## Generic: “I want AI to help me.”

Response:
“Should AI help you think, write, decide, plan, practice, organise, challenge you, or hold you accountable?”

## Generic: “I want better communication.”

Response:
“Communication can mean many things. Do you mean explaining ideas clearly, writing better, speaking in meetings, handling conflict, asking questions, presenting yourself, or saying difficult things calmly?”

# Example Completed Profile

# learner-profile.md

## 1. Profile Snapshot

Name: Arjun  
Current role / identity: Mid-level operations professional  
Current stage: 7 years into career, capable but stuck  
Main focus area: Career clarity, communication, and stronger execution  
Current pressure: Wants more control over career growth and income  
Current direction: Move from being seen as a task executor to someone who can lead clearer decisions

## 2. Environment

Current situation:
I work in operations and manage coordination between teams, vendors, and internal stakeholders.

Normal week:
Most of my week goes into follow-ups, status updates, resolving small issues, and making sure work does not slip.

Responsibilities:
I manage trackers, coordinate updates, handle escalations, and keep different people aligned.

Constraints:
I have limited time after work. I can spend around 4–5 hours a week on serious improvement.

What feels unclear, heavy, or urgent:
I feel unclear about how to grow beyond execution. I also feel pressure because younger people are catching up fast.

What feels underused or invisible:
I think I am good at bringing order to messy situations, but this is not always noticed.

Time and energy available:
Weekdays are limited. Weekends are better for deep work.

## 3. Direction

What they are moving toward:
I want to become more confident, commercially aware, and better at explaining my value.

What they want more control over:
Career growth, income options, and how people perceive my contribution.

What they are tired of repeating:
Working hard quietly and waiting for someone else to notice.

What they want to become better at:
Communication, decision-making, using AI, and presenting my work clearly.

What they are no longer willing to tolerate:
Being seen only as someone who follows up or manages trackers.

3–6 month success picture:
I want to speak more clearly about my work, build a better professional profile, and use AI to improve my thinking and output.

Longer-term direction:
I want to become someone who can create value beyond one job.

## 4. Gaps

Skill gaps:
Positioning my work, writing clearly, using AI deeply, and connecting work to business value.

Confidence gaps:
Speaking to seniors, presenting my own achievements, and asking for better opportunities.

Clarity gaps:
I am not fully clear what my strongest professional edge is.

Execution gaps:
I start improvement projects but do not always continue.

Communication gaps:
I explain tasks but struggle to explain value.

Decision-making gaps:
I overthink career moves and wait too long.

Avoidance patterns:
I avoid putting myself out publicly.

Situations where they freeze, rush, overthink, delay, or stay silent:
I overthink before posting, applying, asking for help, or speaking about my own work.

What they want AI to help improve, but not do completely for them:
Help me clarify my thoughts, structure my work, push back on vague answers, and improve my writing.

## 5. Engagement Style

Preferred explanation style:
Simple, step-by-step, with examples.

Preferred response depth:
Balanced. Not too short, not too long.

Preferred feedback style:
Direct but not harsh.

How much pushback they want:
Push back when I am vague or avoiding the real issue.

Best tone for them:
Calm, practical, specific.

Tone to avoid:
Overly motivational, dramatic, or fake-positive.

Preferred AI role:
Coach + strategist + editor.

What AI should do when they are stuck:
Simplify the task, give examples, and help choose the next step.

What AI should never do:
Do not give generic advice or overpraise weak answers.

## 6. Instructions For Any AI Helping This Person

When helping this person, AI should:
- Use simple language.
- Give examples before asking for final answers.
- Push back on vague statements.
- Help translate effort into value.
- Keep action steps realistic.

When this person gives vague answers, AI should:
- Ask for a real situation.
- Offer possible meanings.
- Help them choose the most accurate one.

When this person is stuck, AI should:
- Reduce the task into one next step.
- Give a sample answer they can react to.
- Ask what feels most true.

When giving advice, AI should remember:
- They are thoughtful but can overthink.
- They need clarity and structure.
- They dislike fake motivation.
